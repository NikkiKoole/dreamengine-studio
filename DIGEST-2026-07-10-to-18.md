## The big arcs

### 1. The worldgen ladder finished — and became drivable (Jul 10)
The multi-week procedural-city program paid off in one day.
- **citygrow rungs 4–7**: district faces extracted from the arterial graph (planar half-edge walk), each face filled with one of five street morphs (grid / organic / cul-de-sac / superblock) by density, then stitched into a whole-city network. **The gate:** T-junction share climbed **1.1% → 64.6%** — real Dutch-city territory (Rotterdam ~60%).
- **Rotterdam calibration (rung 5)**: one DENDRICITY knob unifies the patterns; five SNDi metrics land dead-on vs Rotterdam. `sndi-check --compare` becomes a real PASS/FAIL gate (calibrated citygrow passes 0/8, arterials-only fails 5/8).
- **citygen.h**: the calibrated grammar extracted to a cart-land library header (rungs 2–5, byte-identical), with a `citygen_road_at()` drive-seam.
- **sloop DRIVES it (press M)**: the rig drops into a generated, SNDi-calibrated city — streets with grip on/off, then **buildings** (rung 7) lining arterials + minors, colliding on their real orientation (fixed a SAT bug where long diagonal terraces collided as axis-aligned boxes).

### 2. The junction grammar — roadkit.h (Jul 10)
streetlab's at-grade geometry extracted to a shared, pure, N-arm-native header, then consumed:
- **B2** curb-return fillet + corner solver from a bearings array (not streetlab's `legs[]`).
- **B3** the N-arm field renderer (`rk_field_build`/`rk_field_road`, space-agnostic); **citydrive** draws curb-return junctions through it in ground metres (toggle `J`).
- **B4** the interchange grammar (ramp splines + `rk_make_junction`); **citygrow** renders grade-2 nodes as real cloverleafs (`I` key), fixed a 1.3s zoom-ring stall with `circ_capped()`.
- Every step gated byte-identical: streetlab spec 104/0, mirror-diff 68=68, road-check `--all` PASS.

### 3. The engine learned to hear — the audio-input frontier (Jul 16–18)
The single biggest new capability of the window.
- **mic-spike → Tier-1 mic INPUT seam** (`mic.h` + `mic_desktop.h`): CONFIRMED LIVE, the engine hears (webcam mic, −17 dBFS).
- **`sample_autotune()`** engine primitive — formant-preserving pitch correction — and a whole family on top: **hardtune** (robot T-Pain), **mictune** (focused auto-tune app), **transparent-autotune** (offline PSOLA spike PASSED a real-voice check), and **autotune_mic()** — LIVE streaming PSOLA on the audio thread, phase-locked WSOLA-style.
- Playable mic carts: **singsynth** (voice-as-synth, an honest SK-1), **humseq** (hum → MIDI loop), **voxroll** (Melodyne-style vocal roll), **vocoder v2** (unvoiced/sibilance band so consonants cut through).
- **`DE_MIC_WAV`** harness flag: feed a WAV in as the microphone for headless mic-cart testing.
- **ADR-0032**: live mic effects are live-only.

### 4. Real rigid-body physics — Box2D v3 + a shared Verlet toolkit (Jul 13–14)
- **physics.h** (Jul 13): a tiny shared Verlet toolkit (`PhysPt` + integrate/link/collide/aim) extracted from carts that each rolled their own. Migrated onto it: verlet, tombola, linerider, coaster, tentacle (bone-driven textured mesh), jelly (pressurized soft-body).
- **Box2D v3.1.1 vendored** (Jul 14, pure C, opt-in per-target build) proven by four carts: **tumble** (slingshot, 306-crate stress test, wrecking ball), **buggy** (hill-climb, wheel joints), **silverball** (pinball, motored joints), and **waterjar** (code-first PBD/SPH fluid → dithered metaball liquid).

### 5. The device-face paradigm & the candy rack (Jul 13–15)
- **device-face paradigm** (Jul 13): a reusable deep-instrument UI shape — one machine fills the screen, knobs edit the selected step (parameter locks). Prototyped in **acidface**, documented, worked faces in chordblossom/pocketbox.
- **A control vocabulary** (Jul 14): a beveled-lo-fi look with a one-source top-left (Win95) lighting rule + 3 swappable skins, filled out in an afternoon — **rotaries, switches, indicators, pads, faders, labels, grooveface**.
- **Shared voice banks extracted**: **acid303.h** (both voicings, one file), **tr808.h**, **tr909.h** (faithful banks — the acid303 move for drums).
- **acidcandy** — the candy rack: a five-face device-face instrument (two 303s, blue 808 grid, amber 909, MST master), one shared transport, note-bars you draw + a flag palette (ACC/SLD/TIE/OCT/LEN) you paint on. Iterated hard through Jul 15–17 (visualized flags, free-draw melody, drawable PCF lane, drum drag-paint, per-step p-locks, a nasty drag-release bounce-mute fix).

### 9. The book, and the end-fade fix (Jul 10–12)
