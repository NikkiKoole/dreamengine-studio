# worldgen-plan — the realistic procedural world generator

STATUS: BUILDING (2026-07-06) — rungs 0–1 SHIPPED (the SNDi oracle `sndi-check.js`; the
`runtime/worldnet.h` seam — sloop drives the spine). Rung 2 (the density field, in the `citygrow`
bench) is next; rungs expand Track-A items A2–A4 of
[`driving-world-program.md`](driving-world-program.md)'s sequenced build order.

The plan of attack for closing the program's named gap: **we don't yet have *realistic* procedural
roadgen** — roadnet2's lattice + jitter + β-skeleton reads as "procedural grid," not "a place that
grew." This doc inventories what exists, names what's missing, picks the methods (from the verified
research in [`road-hierarchy-notes.md`](road-hierarchy-notes.md) §8), and lays the gated rung
ladder. Sits under [`driving-world-program.md`](driving-world-program.md) (the umbrella; its §"The
missing capability" is the problem statement) and refines the Phase-2 frontier of
[`road-program-state.md`](road-program-state.md) + the street-generation half of
[`roadnet2-plan.md`](roadnet2-plan.md).

## The gap (what's missing or not great)

1. **The settlement tier reads mechanical** — no *population-density field*; presence/rank come from
   pure hash on a lattice. Both pillar papers drive everything from a density map.
2. **No district concept** — one pattern fills the whole view (streetlab AND v1's L2). The real thing
   is per-region `(pattern, params)` stitched at boundaries — the named, unstarted Phase 2.
3. **The generators are placeholders** — the β-skeleton is elegant but density-blind; streetlab's
   patterns are proven *distinguishable* but generated by a toy morph on a fixed grid.
4. **The validation loop is half-built** — SNDi computes nothing over a `.rvb` or a generated graph
   dump; the per-pattern numeric *target table* is an open research question.
5. **An unwritten architectural tension** — Parish-Müller L-systems and Chen streamline tracing are
   **sequential, global growth algorithms**; roadnet2's virtue is **local, hash-based,
   query-anywhere determinism**. The papers cannot be ported naively.

## The method (the calls)

- **Tensor fields (Chen 2008) for arterials** — chosen *because* a tensor field is **locally
  evaluable**: blend radial-around-centre + grid-aligned-to-entering-highway (v1 already computed
  `city_dir`) + terrain-gradient + noise, deterministic at any point. Only streamline *tracing* is
  sequential — fine, **because a city is bounded**: generate one city's arterial graph wholesale on
  first touch, seeded by its cell hash, cached. Chunk-safe. (`probabletrain` on itch.io is a
  readable open implementation to crib from.)
- **From Parish-Müller, take the split, not the L-system** — `globalGoals`/`localConstraints` and
  the **density map as master input** (where settlements sit, how big, where arterials grow, where
  streets *stop*).
- **The morph-knob synthesis** (notes §8.5) — pattern-bias field · density threshold · dendricity
  knob · curvature knob. streetlab's five patterns become *presets* of these knobs, not separate code paths.
- **Hierarchy by construction** — keep roadnet2's suppression lattice as the deterministic
  *candidate* layer; weight/cull/size candidates by the density field. **Lattice for determinism,
  field for organicness.**
- **Realism is a number** — the generator is realistic when its SNDi profile matches a real city's
  (the umbrella's "OSM as oracle" — no ML).

## Beyond roads — seas, lakes, rivers, rails (the networks generalization)

The maker's ask (2026-07-06): think water + rails into the plan, not roads-only. This *answers*
umbrella open fork #2 — the abstraction is **networks**, and the deeper unity is that the machinery
above already covers them. The world has **one terrain, several typed networks over it, and a
crossing grammar where networks meet.** Field note 004 predicted exactly this.

**What each one *is* in this architecture:**

- **Seas & lakes are not a network — they're a field.** The sea already falls out of the heightmap
  (`P_sea` threshold in roadnet2); lakes are the same mask inland (a deterministic depression/noise
  mask, not global flood-fill). Their real role is as **input to rung 2's density field**: real
  cities grow at coasts, river mouths, confluences and fords — so water adjacency is a density
  *attractor* (harbour cities) while water itself is a *constraint* (density 0, roads need bridges —
  the `passable()`/valley-bridge machinery already handles this). Named bodies ("IJssel Lake") are a
  cheap flavor layer on top.
- **Rivers are the rung-3 tracer with a different field.** An arterial is a streamline of a tensor
  field; a river is a **streamline of the terrain gradient** (downhill), traced from spring
  candidates on the suppression lattice until it reaches sea/lake, class rising with accumulated
  flow (stream → river → major). Same bounded-trace determinism, same edge/graph data model, ~zero
  new machinery. roadnet2's rivers today are cosmetic terrain; this promotes them to **network
  edges you can bridge, follow, and ford** — and they feed back into rung 2 (mouths/confluences =
  city sites) and rung 3 (canal-grid districts, the Delft look citydrive already renders from OSM).
- **Rails are a third network with a stricter grammar.** Same graph model, different constraints:
  **minimum curve radius + gentle grades** (alignment = roadnet2's terrain-aware `link_path` with
  rail constraints — cuts/viaducts where roads would bend), **β-skeleton over city pairs** for the
  mainline topology (the exact hub machinery, sparser), **stations** as the city-edge nodes the
  street tier grows toward, and junctions that are *switches and wyes, not intersections*.
- **The crossing grammar is where the richness lives** — networks meeting is a small typed table,
  and each cell is a *visible, drivable* feature: road×river = bridge/ford/ferry · rail×river =
  viaduct · road×rail = **level crossing** (barriers, a GTA set-piece and a traffic-ai event) or
  bridge by class · rail×road(motorway) = always grade-separated (the continuity tenet generalizes).
- **Bridges follow rules — they try to be SHORT** (maker's rule, 2026-07-06). A bridge crosses at
  the **local narrowest point, roughly perpendicular** to the water/valley, and the *road* pays the
  detour to reach it — in cost terms, a bridge metre costs ≫ a road metre, so the crossing point is
  chosen first and the alignment bends toward it (never "the road happens to hit the river here, so
  a long diagonal bridge appears"). Class modulates it: a motorway tolerates a longer span before
  detouring; a dirt road fords instead. Two follow-ons for free: **crossings concentrate traffic →
  bridgeheads are density attractors** (rung 2 — it's *why* cities sit at fords and
  lowest-bridgeable points), and few crossings per river = the natural chokepoints traffic-ai and
  chases want. roadnet2's `link_path` already carries the seed (`MAX_BRIDGE`, valley
  classification, bend-to-cross); this promotes it from a length cap to a **narrowest-perpendicular
  crossing-site search**, and rail obeys the same rule with a bigger span budget (viaducts).

**Where it hooks into the ladder** (not a separate project):
- **Before rung 1:** `road_at()`'s data model carries a **network/edge-type field** (this section is
  why — decided now, so the seam never needs breaking). A future `net_at()` may generalize the query;
  the field comes first.
- **Rung 2 absorbs water:** the density field takes coast/river/confluence attractors; lakes land as
  part of the terrain mask work.
- **Rung 3 absorbs rivers:** the streamline tracer is written once, parameterized by field
  (eigenvector = street, gradient = river).
- **Rung 6 absorbs the crossing table:** junction emission gains the cross-network cells.
- **Rung 8 — rails (new, after rung 4):** mainline over city pairs + stations + level crossings.
  Needs cities (rung 2–3) to connect; doesn't block anything before it.
- **The OSM oracle extends for free:** `railway=*` / `waterway=*` are ordinary OSM tags — the
  importer + `sndi-check` measure real rail/water networks the same way (rail curvature stats are
  the calibration target for the rail grammar).

## Where the code lives (decided 2026-07-06)

**Rungs 2–5 get a dedicated bench cart — `citygrow` — separate from `roadnet2`.** The repo's proven
pattern (streetlab/roadlab/lotfill/interiors): each research question gets its own knobbed,
spec-lockable sandbox, developed in isolation, then consumed. The generation grammar is exactly
cart-shaped research — morph-knob sliders, seed reroll, instant visual feedback — and iterating on
it inside roadnet2 would put every experiment on the spine's critical path. The split is clean
because **a city is bounded**: the bench generates ONE city at a time (density field → arterials →
districts → fill) with a **live SNDi panel + an OSM target loaded beside it for A/B** — rungs 2–5's
calibration loop as an interactive instrument. `roadnet2` stays the infinite deterministic
substrate that later *calls* the proven generator per city cell. (Bonus, real in this repo:
rung 1 in `roadnet2.c` and rungs 2–5 in `citygrow.c` touch disjoint files — parallel agents don't
collide. The existing `worldgen` cart was checked and is a noise-terrain/camera toy — not reused.)

**The one guard, non-negotiable — ONE data model.** This is how v1 died (two street
representations diverged). The bench must emit **the same typed graph the spine consumes** (the
common model, edge-type field included), and the generator code is **fenced from day one** for
extraction into a library header — the discipline that made streetlab's OSM-ingest lift cleanly
toward roadkit. The bench is a *petri dish for the grammar*, never a second home for world state.

**The extraction rung — `citygen.h` (between rungs 5 and 6):** once the grammar passes rung-5
calibration, extract it per [ADR-0006](../decisions/0006-library-carts-not-engine.md) and have
`roadnet2` call it per city — the roadkit trigger discipline ("extract when a second consumer
needs it, designed from a working one"). `citygrow` then stays the tuning bench, spec-locked.
