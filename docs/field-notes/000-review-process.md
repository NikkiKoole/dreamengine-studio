Review 0000 — Why this review exists
Context

The original goal of this review was to read the DreamEngine repository and produce a final report.

After the first review session it became clear that this approach would lose much of the value of the process.

The most valuable insights were not found by simply reading documents. They emerged through conversation, hypothesis, correction, and reflection.

Examples include:

The North Star changed during the review.
DreamEngine is evolving from a public fantasy console into a personal creative instrument.
The "Promotion Principle" applies not only to APIs but also to workflows and tooling.
The idea of Context Assembly emerged naturally from discussing agent workflows.
The Honeybee metaphor better described the creator's role than earlier metaphors.

These ideas did not exist explicitly in the repository before the review.

Decision

The review itself becomes part of the DreamEngine knowledge base.

Instead of producing one large report at the end, the review is performed as a sequence of small review notes.

Each review note captures one important realization.

The final report, if one is produced, becomes a synthesis of these notes rather than replacing them.

Review workflow

Each session follows three phases:

Explore

Read documents, inspect carts, discuss ideas, ask questions, challenge assumptions.

Distill

Whenever a significant realization emerges, capture it as a review note.

Continue

Future sessions begin by reading previous review notes before continuing exploration.

Principles
The review may change its own methodology.
Hypotheses are welcome and may later prove wrong.
Corrections are valuable and become part of the historical record.
The review documents both the project and the evolving understanding of the project.
Discoveries created during conversation are treated as first-class knowledge.
Expected outcome

The review becomes another artifact of DreamEngine.

Rather than describing a finished project from the outside, it documents the co-evolution of the project and the understanding of its creators.



Don't ever edit a field note to make it "perfect."

Only:

fix typos,
clarify wording,
add a small update at the bottom if reality changed.

Don't rewrite history.

If six months from now we discover Context Assembly was the wrong direction, don't rewrite 002-context-assembly.md.

Instead write:

017-context-assembly-revisited.md

or

023-knowledge-assembly.md
