# 005 — Evolution through Inflection Points

> This note captures a discovery made during the evolution of DreamEngine.
>
> It records our understanding at the time it was written.
> Later notes may refine, extend or replace it.

**Status**
Observed

**Date**
2026-06-26

**Confidence**
High

---

## Observation

DreamEngine rarely changes direction because of long-term planning.

Instead, its evolution is driven by a series of inflection points where a single experiment proves an entirely new capability.

These experiments rarely become the final destination.

Instead, they permanently expand what becomes possible afterwards.

---

## Why this matters

The most important carts are not necessarily the most polished or most successful.

They are the carts that changed the creator's understanding of what DreamEngine could become.

After an inflection point, new families of carts naturally emerge.

The experiment acts as proof that a previously uncertain direction is now viable.

---

## Types of inflection points

Several kinds of inflection points appear to exist.

### Capability

A cart proves a previously difficult technical area is achievable.

Example directions include advanced audio synthesis and physical modelling.

---

### World

A cart demonstrates that a larger type of world or simulation may be possible.

Rather than improving a single game, it expands the project's horizon.

---

### Workflow

A cart changes how DreamEngine itself is developed.

Examples include external data pipelines, generated assets and OpenStreetMap integration.

---

### Creative

A cart changes the relationship between imagination and implementation.

Coaster is an example, transforming a child's drawing into a working simulation and opening entirely new creative possibilities.

---

## Implications

DreamEngine's history is better understood through its inflection points than through a chronological list of carts.

These moments explain why later systems exist.

Rather than asking:

> Which carts are the best?

it may be more useful to ask:

> Which carts permanently changed the future of DreamEngine?
