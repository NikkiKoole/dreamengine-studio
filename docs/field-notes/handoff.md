# Handoff for the Next Research Partner

If you are reading this, you are continuing an ongoing research journal about DreamEngine.

Please resist the urge to immediately generate new insights.

First, understand the notebook.

---

## What this folder is

This is not project documentation.

It is not a design specification.

It is a research journal.

Each field note captures a moment where our understanding of DreamEngine changed.

The goal is to preserve those changes in understanding.

Not to prove we were right.

---

## Before writing anything

Read in roughly this order:

1. What is this.md
2. 000-review-process.md
3. 001-protocol.md
4. 100-first-synthesis.md

Only then begin reading individual field notes.

This provides the vocabulary and methodology used throughout the notebook.

---

## The role of the researcher

Do not try to explain DreamEngine.

Instead, ask:

> "What don't we understand yet?"

Good field notes begin with genuine uncertainty.

---

## Standards for new field notes

A new field note should satisfy all of the following:

* It changes our understanding of the project.
* It is supported by evidence from the repository or experiments.
* It cannot be expressed as a small edit to an existing note.
* It is focused on a single observation.

If these conditions are not met, continue researching instead of writing.

---

## Prefer observation over certainty

When possible:

* write "Observation"
* write "Hypothesis"
* write "Working Theory"

Avoid writing conclusions unless they have repeatedly survived attempts to disprove them.

---

## Preserve history

Do not rewrite old field notes because a newer understanding exists.

Write a new one.

The evolution of understanding is part of the research.

---

## Respect the Promotion Principle

DreamEngine promotes ideas only after repeated evidence.

This notebook follows the same rule.

Interesting conversations are not automatically field notes.

Interesting field notes are not automatically syntheses.

Ideas should earn promotion.

---

## Your task

Your job is not to make the notebook larger.

Your job is to make it more accurate.

Sometimes that means writing a new field note.

Sometimes that means concluding that no new note is needed.

Both are valuable outcomes.

---

## Remember

The purpose of this notebook is not to describe DreamEngine.

It is to describe how our understanding of DreamEngine evolves.

If you help preserve that process, you are contributing to the project itself.
