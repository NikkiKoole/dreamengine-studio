# The game you're building: CHESS

## Premise
A complete, legal game of **chess** on one 8×8 board. Two-player **hotseat** by
default, or **vs CPU** — a shallow **minimax (depth 3)** with simple material +
position evaluation. Every rule that matters is in: all piece movement, captures,
**castling**, **en passant**, **pawn promotion** (to queen), and full
**check / checkmate / stalemate** detection. Click a piece, its legal targets light
up, click one to move. It's a quiet, deliberate board game — no clocks, no clutter.

## The locked slice (build exactly this)
- Full legal move generation for all six piece types, including the three special
  moves: castling (king + rook, with the can't-castle-through/out-of-check rules),
  en passant, and pawn promotion (auto-queen — promotion to other pieces is cut).
- A move is only legal if it doesn't leave **your own king in check** — generated by
  make-move / scan-attacks / unmake-move on a single board array.
- **Check / checkmate / stalemate** detection drives the end state and the HUD.
- **vs CPU**: negamax with alpha-beta to depth 3, material + a small piece-square
  bias, picking the CPU's reply after the human moves. Hotseat is the default; a
  title-screen toggle picks the mode and the human's color vs the CPU.
- **Highlight legal moves** for the picked piece (dots on empty targets, rings on
  captures); show check with a red king square.

### Cut (do not build)
Clocks/timers, opening books, draw by repetition / 50-move / insufficient material,
under-promotion, threefold, move list / PGN, network play.

## Engine features it leans into (and why)
- **Primitives over sprites.** The board, the highlights, and the pieces are all
  drawn from `rectfill`/`circfill`/`tri`/`line` + a glyph letter. A chess set is
  geometric and two-tone; a 64-slot sprite sheet would be more work and less crisp.
  No `.cart.js`. (Pieces use a compact vector-ish silhouette per type, two colors.)
- **Mouse-primary input** (`mouse_pressed`, `mouse_x/y`) — click to select, click to
  move — with the board mapped straight to screen squares. Keyboard `R` restarts,
  `M`/`ESC` back to the menu.
- **`pal()` / palette indices** for the two armies (warm ivory vs cold slate) and the
  light/dark board squares — readable, no art assets.
- **Sound where it earns it**: a soft wood `note()` tick on a move, a sharper hit on a
  capture, a low buzz on an illegal click, a small fanfare `strum()`/`chord()` on
  checkmate. Silence otherwise — chess wants calm.
- **`save_int`** records a running tally (wins per side / games played) so the session
  has a tiny bit of memory.
- Light **juice**: the picked square pulses (`sin_deg`), a captured piece does a quick
  fade-out, a `shake` on checkmate.

## Controls
Mouse: click one of your pieces to select it (legal targets highlight — dots on empty
squares, rings on captures); click a highlighted square to move; click the piece again
or an empty non-target square to deselect. Title screen: click **HOTSEAT** or **VS
CPU** (and pick a side). In game: **R** new game, **M** / **ESC** back to the menu.
