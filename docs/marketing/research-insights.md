# Research insights — what the CHORDESTA thread teaches our launch

> Distilled from [`raw/omnichord-chordesta-thread.md`](raw/omnichord-chordesta-thread.md) (a free
> browser/Android Omnichord posted to r/synthesizers — almost exactly our plan). These are the
> strategy edits it forces; the load-bearing ones are already applied to
> [`tinyjam-marketing.md`](tinyjam-marketing.md) and [`tinyjam-launch-strumharp.md`](tinyjam-launch-strumharp.md).

## The big one: our draft copy was written in the style that got flamed

The thread's title is literally *"couldn't afford an omnichord, this is what happened."* Our Strumharpy
copy opened with the *same* pitch ("obsessed with the Omnichord… can't justify the price… so I made…").
That framing is now a **burned cliché** the community reads as AI-marketing snake oil. **Killed it.**

**The AI-marketing tells they named (avoid all of them):**
- the "I couldn't afford X so I built it" origin story
- short punchy sentences + a clean problem→solution arc (reads as templated persuasion)
- relatability asides ("I have a family")
- name-dropping a pile of technologies / effects
- **"free to *try*"** — hiding that it's paid; "purposely left off the price" was the most-cited tell
- a fresh / sock-puppet account; an obviously-"Promoted" ad

## The lessons

1. **Guilty until proven innocent.** Music subs are flooded with low-effort free plugins; a self-posted
   "I made an instrument app" triggers an immune response by default. Wording alone can't fix this —
   the real mitigations are **(a)** a real account with history, **(b)** demo quality, **(c)** radical
   transparency.
2. **Lead with the artifact, not the story.** The people who *tried* CHORDESTA liked it ("sounds
   beautiful", "my daughter had a blast"). The product wins; the narrative loses. Open with a *loop you
   made* + a genuine, specific question. Minimal origin story.
3. **Be transparent about the commercial intent up front.** Hiding the paywall ("free to try") was the
   #1 trust-killer. Our honest arc (free web demo now, paid iOS app later) is fine — *state it plainly*,
   don't pose as a pure free gift then paywall. One upfront line defuses it.
4. **Don't drop builds into hostile threads.** A commenter offering a GPT-made VST3 got −12 + "Fuck
   off." Unsolicited AI-made binaries = instant rejection. Share a *playable link*, in the right place,
   in gift-tone — never a "here's my .vst3" drive-by.
5. **The own-engine angle is a credibility asset.** Strumharpy runs on a homemade fantasy-console
   engine, not a Vercel/AI template. One honest line about that signals real craft and *counters* the
   vibe-coding suspicion — but state it once; don't tech-name-drop.
6. **Open-source norm tension.** This scene half-expects "share the code." We're shipping a *commercial*
   product, so we won't — which is exactly why honesty about being a commercial product (lesson 3)
   matters more than posing as a community freebie. The free *web demo* is the goodwill, not the source.

## Competitive landscape (named by the thread — verify/keep current)

The omnichord-app space is **populated**, so Strumharpy must differentiate (the harpy mascot + the
pixel/lo-fi identity + iOS polish are the differentiators):

| Thing | What | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **minichord** (minichord.com) | open-source *hardware* | beloved, hackable; the diagonal touch-plate mod is praised. The community's darling. |
| **Chordion** (olympianoiseco.com) | **iOS** app | the repeatedly-named iOS option — our most direct iOS competitor. Study it. |
| **MidiStrum** (f-droid) | open-source Android, MIDI-out | "solid", pairs with Dexed. |
| **CHORDESTA / web "Harpsichord"** | the thread's app | the web version was someone's *favorite* — a web omnichord can win on feel. |

**iOS is the thin spot.** The OP couldn't do iOS ("no Mac"); demand for an iOS version was explicit and
unmet (Chordion aside). **That's our lane** (the whole iOS plan).

## Naming check

"HarpiChord" got store-autocorrected to "HarpiSchord" (harpsichord) → forced a rebrand. **Strumharpy**
is distinctive with no real-instrument collision — but still **search it in the App Store before
locking** (autocorrect + existing-app collisions).
