cr-78 compurhythm

🟡 Mostly Playable — works, with rough edges

All 15 voices of the Roland CR-78 (1978) rebuilt from their analog circuits with the synth API: damped-sine membranes (kick/bongos/congas), one noise source shaped per voice by different filters (the famous swishy bandpassed hats, highpassed maracas, resonant tambourine jingle), detuned square pairs/triples for cowbell and the metallic beat, and the guiro's ratcheting scrape — bandpassed noise chopped by a 36Hz volume LFO under a rising cutoff sweep. INSTR_FM showcase - the third modeled ENGINE: two-operator FM, the DX-synth recipe. re-201 space echo

🟠 Rough on Mobile — technically runs, expect pain

Roland's RE-201 (1974) — a free-running tape loop, a record head, and an INTENSITY knob that feeds the tape back into itself; the box that made echo an instrument. Showcase cart for THE echo bus (the engine has exactly one, by design): a guitar string (INSTR_PLUCK) plays into the chamber, and five knobs map straight onto the new API — RATE is echo() time and the headline trick: sweep it while a tail rings and the repeats PITCH-BEND, because the bus's read tap glides like varying tape speed.
